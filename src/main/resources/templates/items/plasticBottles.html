How to dispose of plastic bottles


The deposit return scheme for Ireland

This is a new bottle and can recycling system.
Under this scheme, most drinks containers made from plastic, aluminum, ranging from 150ml and 3liters, are included.
These eligible containers will feature the Re-turn logo, facilitating easy identification for consumers.
Retailers across the country will accept empty containers.

At the shops you can either return the containers:
Using a Reverse Vending Machine (RVM)
Manually in the shop

- Containers between 150ml to 500ml: 15 cent deposit
- Containers over 500ml to 3 litres: 25 cent deposit


The legislative regulations for the introduction of this scheme were signed by the Minister for Environment, Climate, and Communications, Eamon Ryan T.D, and it commenced on February 1st, 2024.

Ensure the bottle is completely emptied and rinsed. Leftover food or liquids can contaminate other recyclables, and bottles with remaining liquid may not be recycled due to the automated sorting process considering them too heavy. Liquid can also damage the machinery.

Leave the labels on; they will be removed during processing. To save space, squash the bottles.

Replace lids and tops. If they are already attached, leave them on to ensure they are recycled along with the bottle.
